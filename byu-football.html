1984 National Champions

In 1984, the BYU Cougars football team achieved something no one had done before — and few have matched since. Finishing the season 13–0, BYU was awarded the UPI National Championship, cementing their place in college football history as the only school outside the traditional powerhouse conferences to claim an undisputed title in the modern era.

Head coach LaVell Edwards had built a pass-first, innovative offense that opponents simply couldn't stop. Quarterback Robbie Bosco led the charge, throwing for over 3,800 yards on the season despite suffering a serious ankle injury in the Holiday Bowl — and playing through it anyway to secure the win.

  1. Regular Season Dominance
    • Went 11–0 heading into bowl season
    • Outscored opponents by an average of 22 points per game
    • Led the nation in passing offense
  2. Holiday Bowl Victory
    • Defeated Michigan 24–17 on December 21, 1984
    • Bosco played through an ankle injury to lead the comeback
    • BYU named #1 in final UPI Poll
  3. LaVell Edwards Legacy
    • Coach Edwards finished with a 257–101 career record
    • Inducted into the College Football Hall of Fame in 2004
    • LaVell Edwards Stadium named in his honor

BYU plays at LaVell Edwards Stadium, Provo, Utah — capacity 63,470

The Move to the Big 12

🏟️

A New Era

After decades as an independent and years in the WCC for other sports, BYU officially joined the Big 12 Conference for all sports beginning in the 2023–24 academic year. It marked the biggest realignment move in program history.

⚡

Why It Matters

The Big 12 brings BYU into a Power Four conference for the first time, meaning improved recruiting pipelines, nationally televised games on ESPN and Fox, and a legitimate path to the College Football Playoff.

🎯

On the Field

BYU has shown it can compete at the highest level, facing off against Texas, Oklahoma State, Kansas State, and other traditional powers each season — bringing the Cougar fan base to new heights of excitement.
